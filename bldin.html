Blood Groups Information


Welcome to the Blood Groups Information page. Blood groups are categorized based on the presence or absence of specific antigens and antibodies in the blood. Understanding blood groups is essential for safe blood transfusions and organ transplants.


A Positive (A+)

A Positive blood type has the A antigen and the Rh factor. It is one of the most common blood types and can receive A+, A-, O+, and O- blood types.

B Positive (B+)

B Positive blood type has the B antigen and the Rh factor. It can receive B+, B-, O+, and O- blood types.

AB Positive (AB+)

AB Positive blood type has both A and B antigens and the Rh factor. It is known as the universal recipient, able to receive all blood types.

O Positive (O+)

O Positive blood type has no A or B antigens but has the Rh factor. It can donate to A+, B+, AB+, and O+ blood types.

A Negative (A-)

A Negative blood type has the A antigen but lacks the Rh factor. It can receive A- and O- blood types.

B Negative (B-)

B Negative blood type has the B antigen but lacks the Rh factor. It can receive B- and O- blood types.

AB Negative (AB-)

AB Negative blood type has both A and B antigens but lacks the Rh factor. It can receive AB-, A-, B-, and O- blood types.

O Negative (O-)

O Negative blood type has no A or B antigens and lacks the Rh factor. It is known as the universal donor, able to donate to all blood types.
